Answers to the Questions
Investors Actually Ask

Is Dubai real estate a good investment in 2026? +
Yes — with qualification. Dubai's real estate market remains one of the world's best-performing for risk-adjusted returns, driven by population growth, zero capital gains tax, strong rental demand, and continued infrastructure development. The key distinction in 2026 is selectivity: not all segments, areas, or price points perform equally. The right assets — underwritten correctly — continue to deliver 8–14% total returns. The wrong ones can underperform. Selective advisory is essential.
What is the average rental yield in Dubai? +
Gross rental yields in Dubai range from 5% to 14% depending on asset class, location, and management strategy. Studios in JVC and JLT regularly deliver 10–12% gross. 1BR apartments in Dubai Marina and Business Bay: 7–9%. Villas in premium areas: 4–6% gross but with higher capital appreciation. Net yields after service charges, management fees, and DEWA typically run 1.5–2.5% below gross. Our advisory focuses on maximising net yield, not headline gross numbers.
Where should I invest $1M in Dubai real estate? +
At the $1M (approximately AED 3.7M) level, three strategies work in 2026: (1) Off-plan in a supply-constrained emerging area — targeting 40–60% capital appreciation at handover. (2) Two yield-generating apartments in high-demand rental corridors for income from day one. (3) A single premium ready unit in an established area with strong resale liquidity. The right choice depends entirely on your return objective, timeline, and tax situation. This is precisely what a private consultation maps out.
Can foreigners buy property in Dubai? +
Yes. Foreign nationals can purchase property in Dubai's designated freehold zones — which include the most commercially valuable areas: Downtown, Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, JVC, Business Bay, MBR City, Dubai Hills, and more. There are no restrictions on nationality, no required residency, and no annual property tax. Ownership is registered with the Dubai Land Department and is legally protected. A property purchase of AED 750,000+ also qualifies investors for a UAE Golden Visa.
What are the risks of investing in Dubai real estate? +
Key risks include: developer non-delivery on off-plan projects (mitigated by selecting RERA-escrow-compliant developers with proven track records), market oversupply in specific segments (currently most acute in mid-tier apartment stock), currency risk (AED is pegged to USD, reducing FX risk for USD-based investors), and geopolitical risk (low historically but not zero). The risk-mitigation framework applied across all advisory mandates addresses each of these systematically before any capital is committed.
How does off-plan property investment work in Dubai? +
Off-plan means purchasing a property before construction is completed, directly from the developer. The typical structure: 20–30% down payment at signing, instalments during construction (every 3–6 months), and the balance at handover. The investor advantage: launch pricing is typically 15–30% below projected market value at completion. Risk: construction delays and developer financial health. Mitigation: select developers with RERA-regulated escrow accounts and a documented delivery track record. The best deals are never publicly marketed — they go to advisory networks first.
